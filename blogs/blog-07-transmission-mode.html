Transmission Modes in Computer Networks

Networking January 02, 2026 Jobaer Hossain

Introduction

Transmission mode refers to the mechanism of transferring data between two devices connected over a network. There are three types of transmission modes:

  • Simplex Mode
  • Half-Duplex Mode
  • Full-Duplex Mode

Simplex Mode

In Simplex mode, communication is unidirectional. Only one of the two devices can transmit; the other can only receive.

  • Direction: One-way only
  • Example: Keyboard to computer, TV broadcast, radio station
  • Advantages:
    • Simple and cost-effective
    • Entire bandwidth used for transmission in one direction
  • Disadvantages:
    • No bidirectional communication
    • Receiver cannot reply

Half-Duplex Mode

In Half-Duplex mode, both devices can transmit and receive, but not at the same time. Communication is bidirectional but alternating.

  • Direction: Two-way, but one direction at a time
  • Example: Walkie-talkie, two-way radio
  • Advantages:
    • Bidirectional communication possible
    • Cost-effective compared to full-duplex
  • Disadvantages:
    • Lower efficiency (only one direction at a time)
    • Delay due to switching

Full-Duplex Mode

In Full-Duplex mode, both devices can transmit and receive simultaneously. Communication is truly bidirectional.

  • Direction: Two-way simultaneous
  • Example: Telephone conversation, modern Ethernet networks
  • Advantages:
    • Highest efficiency and performance
    • No delay in communication
    • Double the bandwidth utilization
  • Disadvantages:
    • More expensive
    • Complex implementation

Comparison of Transmission Modes

Parameter Simplex Half-Duplex Full-Duplex
Direction of Communication Unidirectional Bidirectional (alternating) Bidirectional (simultaneous)
Data Transmission Only one way Both ways, one at a time Both ways at the same time
Bandwidth Utilization Full in one direction Half (shared) Full (doubled)
Performance Low Moderate High
Examples Keyboard, Monitor, TV broadcast Walkie-talkie Telephone, Internet connection

Summary

The choice of transmission mode depends on the application requirements for speed, cost, and bidirectional communication needs.
