Basics of Computer Networking

Dec 31, 2025 | Networking

Introduction

A computer network is a collection of interconnected devices that share resources and information. These devices can include computers, servers, printers, and other hardware. Networks allow for the efficient exchange of data, enabling applications such as email, file sharing, and internet browsing.

Basic Terminologies

  • Network: A group of connected computers and devices that can communicate and share data.
  • Node: Any device that can send, receive, or forward data (e.g., computers, laptops, mobiles, printers, servers).
  • Networking Devices: Devices like routers, switches, hubs, and access points that manage network functions.
  • Transmission Media:
    • Wired: Ethernet cables, optical fiber
    • Wireless: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, infrared
  • Service Provider Networks: Networks leased from providers (e.g., ISPs, mobile carriers).

Working of a Computer Network

Networks consist of nodes (devices) connected by links (media). Data transfer follows protocols, and each device has a unique IP address.

A firewall monitors traffic and allows, blocks, or drops data based on rules.

Types of Computer Network Architecture

  • Client-Server Architecture: Central server manages client behavior.
  • Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Architecture: No central server; devices act as both client and server.

Network Devices

Key devices that facilitate communication:

  • Router: Connects multiple networks and directs packets.
  • Switch: Connects devices in a network and forwards data to specific recipients.
  • Hub: Broadcasts data to all connected devices.
  • Bridge: Connects and filters traffic between network segments.
  • Gateway: Translates between different network protocols.
  • Access Point (AP): Provides wireless connectivity.
  • Modem: Converts digital signals to analog and vice versa for internet access.
  • Firewall: Controls traffic for security.

Goals of Networks

  • Convenience and efficiency
  • Resource management
  • Security and protection
  • Reliability and fault tolerance
  • Scalability

Uses of Computer Networks

  • Communication (email, chat, video calls)
  • Resource sharing (printers, files)
  • Remote access
  • Collaboration
  • E-commerce
  • Education and online learning

Characteristics of Computer Networks

  • Security: Protection from unauthorized access using firewalls, encryption.
  • Reliability: Availability with redundancy and backups.
  • Scalability: Ability to grow without performance loss.
  • High Performance: Fast transfer, low latency.
  • Quality of Service (QoS): Prioritizes critical data.
