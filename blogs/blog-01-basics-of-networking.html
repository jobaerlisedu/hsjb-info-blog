Basics of Computer Networking

Dec 31, 2025 | Networking

Introduction

A computer network is a collection of interconnected devices that share resources and information. These devices can include computers, servers, printers, and other hardware. Networks allow for the efficient exchange of data, enabling applications such as email, file sharing, and internet browsing.

Basic Terminologies

  • Network: A group of connected computers and devices that can communicate and share data.
  • Node: Any device that can send, receive, or forward data in a network. This includes laptops, mobiles, printers, earbuds, servers, etc.
  • Networking Devices: Devices that manage and support networking functions. This includes routers, switches, hubs, and access points.
  • Transmission Media: The physical or wireless medium through which data travels between devices.
    • Wired media: Ethernet cables, optical fiber
    • Wireless media: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, infrared
  • Service Provider Networks: Networks offered by external providers that allow users or organizations to lease network access and capabilities (e.g., ISPs, mobile carriers).

Working of a Computer Network

The basic building blocks of a Computer network are Nodes and Links.

  • Network Node: Any device in a network, such as a modem, router, or computer, that sends or receives data.
  • Link: The medium that connects nodes, like cables, wires, or wireless signals.

Networks use rules called protocols to send and receive data between devices. Each device is identified by a unique IP Address.

A firewall monitors traffic and allows, blocks, or drops data based on set rules.

Types of Computer Network Architecture

  • Client-Server Architecture: Central server manages client behavior.
  • Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Architecture: No central server; devices act as both client and server.

Network Devices

Key devices that facilitate communication:

  • Router: Connects multiple networks (like a home network to the internet) and directs data packets to their correct destination.
  • Switch: Connects devices within a network (like computers in an office) and forwards data only to the specific device it is meant for.
  • Hub: A basic device that connects multiple devices in a network; it sends data to all devices (less efficient than a switch).
  • Bridge: Connects and filters traffic between two networks or segments, helping to reduce data congestion.
  • Gateway: Connects two different types of networks and translates data between different protocols.
  • Access Point (AP): Provides wireless connectivity to devices and extends a wired network into a Wi-Fi network.
  • Modem: Converts digital data from a computer into signals for phone/cable lines and vice versa, providing internet access.
  • Firewall: Monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic, providing security by blocking unauthorized access.

Goals of Networks

  • Convenience: Makes computer use easier for users.
  • Efficiency: Manages hardware resources effectively for better performance.
  • Resource Management: Allocates CPU, memory, I/O, and storage fairly and efficiently.
  • Security & Protection: Protects data and resources from unauthorized access.
  • Reliability & Fault Tolerance: Ensures system runs smoothly and recovers from failures.
  • Scalability: Supports growth in users, processes, and resources.

Uses of Computer Networks

  • Communication: Email, chat, and video conferencing.
  • Resource Sharing: Share printers, scanners, and files to save cost and effort.
  • Remote Access: Access data and systems from anywhere.
  • Collaboration: Work together on projects, share ideas, and review work.
  • E-commerce: Enables online shopping and secure payments.
  • Education: Supports online learning, research, and studentâ€“teacher collaboration.

Characteristics of Computer Networks

  • Security: Protection from unauthorized access using firewalls, encryption.
  • Reliability: Availability with redundancy and backups.
  • Scalability: Ability to grow without performance loss.
  • High Performance: Fast transfer, low latency.
  • Quality of Service (QoS): Prioritizes critical data.
