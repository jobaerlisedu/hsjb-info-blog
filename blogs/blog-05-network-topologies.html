Types of Network Topology

Jan 02, 2026 | Networking

Introduction

Network topology is the arrangement of nodes and links in a network. It defines how data flows.

Common Topologies

Mesh Topology

Every device connects to every other device via dedicated channels. Robust but expensive.

Star Topology

All devices connect to a central hub. Easy to setup and robust.

Bus Topology

All devices connect to a single backbone cable. Cost-effective for small networks.

Ring Topology

Devices form a ring; data flows in one direction.

Tree Topology

Hierarchical structure combining star and bus topologies.

Hybrid Topology

Combination of two or more different topologies.

Summary

Choosing the right topology affects performance, cost, and reliability. Star is most common today.
